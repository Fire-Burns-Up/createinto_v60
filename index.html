Clients are $30 away from a solution

  • Wix, Squarespace, wordpress.com, portfolio.adobe.com
  • Fiverr, upwork, promo
  • Aliexpress, undercutting
  • Arduino, raspi

If you've been making ads long enough, you've seen this cycle 2 or 3 times

  • Client opts for low-cost
  • Low-cost = ubiquity
  • Ubiquity kills efficacy
  • Low-cost is at odds with quality
  • Cheap solution creates a fake-capitalism
  • Product/vertical dies, something else comes along

But surely a real client would never rely on cheap solutions!?

Ogilvy was right:

  • Take immense pain in selecting your clients
  • Only seek clients with a product or service you are proud of
  • Fire clients at least 5 times more often than you get fired. Always do it for the same reason: if their behavior erodes the morale of the people working on their account. Do not allow this from any client
    - David Ogilvy, paraphrased

Our checklist

  1. Is it cool or sexy
  2. Does it expand our capabilities
  3. Do we have a product that can be used
  4. Do we retain IP
  5. Can we publish/ promote it
  6. Will it be eligible for an award
  7. Is the timeline feasible
  8. Is the budget feasible
  9. Can we counter pitch our ideas
  10. Can we make money
  11. Do we have creative control/ input
  12. Are we not just implementing somebody else’s idea
  13. Is it meaningful
  14. Is it fun
  15. Do we want to kill ourselves reading the RFP
  16. Will it fail
  17. What are the consequences of failure
  18. What is a success
  19. Is it a one-off
  20. Do we need to do it
